Enhancing Writing Skills with AI

By Sarah Mitchell

When looking at the different outputs, I noticed that the tone, word choice, and structure of the messages changed depending on who they were meant for and the level of formality. I used Chat-GPT 4.0 and DeepSeek 1.0 with three different prompts (professional, linked in message and friendly).

The formal email was polite and professional, with a clear structure. It used full sentences and respectful language to make a formal request, which is typical for business communication. For LinkedIn, the tone became a little more conversational, though still professional. It was shorter and more to the point, with phrases like “I’d love to connect” making it more friendly but still appropriate for networking.In the informal message to a friend, the tone was the most relaxed. It had a casual feel, using phrases like “Hey” and “Would you mind passing along my resume?” to make it feel more like a friendly favor. The structure was much looser compared to the other two.

Overall, ChatGPT adjusted the tone, word choice, and format to fit the situation—whether it was a formal email, LinkedIn message, or casual request to a friend—while still getting the same message across. When I tried using DeepSeek - I found it incredibly helpful that it showed what it was changing and why.
